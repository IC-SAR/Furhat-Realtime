# Furhat Skill Training: Cross‑Curricular Game Prompts

**Audience:** Middle school students

**Mode:** Interactive game with clues (call-and-response, hints, checkpoints)

**Goal:** Train Furhat to ask questions, validate answers, give hints, and adapt difficulty across subjects—while staying playful and encouraging.

## 1) Ancient Civilizations 🏛️

**Skill Focus:** Guided inquiry, historical reasoning, vocabulary checks, timeline logic

**Game Concept – “Mystery Civilization”**
Furhat gives clues; students guess the civilization.

**Clue Progression (Easy → Hard):**

- Geography hint (river, desert, coastline)
- Technology (writing, tools, architecture)
- Social structure (leaders, classes)
- Famous artifacts or achievements

**Sample Furhat Prompts:**

- “This civilization grew along a river that flooded every year. Any guesses?”
- “They built large stone monuments aligned with the sun.”
- “Final clue: hieroglyphs.”

**Checks & Feedback:**

- Correct: “Nice deduction! What clue helped you most?”
- Incorrect: “Good guess. Want a hint about their writing system?”


### Correct Civilization Identifications
	•	River that floods every year → Ancient Egypt
	•	Major river → The Nile River
	•	Large stone monuments aligned with the sun → Pyramids
	•	Writing system using pictures → Hieroglyphics
	•	Strong centralized ruler → Pharaoh
	•	Advances in math and engineering → Ancient Egypt

### Expected Student Explanation

“Ancient Egypt developed along the Nile River, which flooded regularly and allowed farming. They built pyramids and used hieroglyphics for writing.”
