## 3) *The Martian* + Space 🚀📘

**Skill Focus:** Reading comprehension, problem‑solving, applied science

**Game Concept – “Survive Like Mark Watney”**
Students choose solutions to space survival problems.

**Scenario Prompts:**

- “You’re stranded on Mars with limited oxygen. What’s your first priority?”
- “How could science help you grow food here?”

**Science Tie‑Ins:**

- Atmosphere differences
- Gravity
- Engineering constraints

**Book Connection Questions:**

- “What problem did Mark solve using chemistry?”
- “Why was communication with Earth difficult?”

**Checks & Feedback:**

- “That’s exactly the kind of thinking Mark used.”
- “Interesting idea—what resource would limit that plan?”

### Survival Scenario Answers
	•	First survival priority on Mars → Oxygen and shelter
	•	Biggest environmental challenge → Thin atmosphere
	•	Why food is difficult to grow → Lack of soil, water, and atmosphere

### Science-Based Answers
	•	How Mark Watney makes water → Combines hydrogen and oxygen
	•	Why communication is difficult → Distance, time delay, damaged equipment
	•	Why problem-solving is critical → Limited resources and isolation


### Core Takeaway

“Science, engineering, and creativity are essential for survival in space.”
